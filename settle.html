Session 1: Artist Settlement – Core Functions

Add Show Revenues, Fees, Taxes

  • Record all gross revenues, tax breakdowns, and show fees.

Add Ticket Stats

  • Enter ticket counts including comps and drops.

Add Standard Expenses

  • Input final actual costs and assign responsibilities.

Add Buyouts and Reimbursements

  • Include meals, travel, and additional artist costs.

Enter Artist Earnings + Deposits

  • Finalize artist earnings and log any previous deposits or payments.

Session 2: Promoter Settlement – Ancillary and Exclusions

Add Ancillary Revenue

  • Include sponsorships, VIP, parking, and other non-ticket income.

Exclude Venue Rent

  • Remove venue rent from promoter-facing settlement calculations.

Session 3: Actual vs Internal Expenses

Differentiate Artist vs Internal Labor

  • Tag labor expenses by visibility to ensure accurate reporting.

Exclude Internal-Only Expenses

  • Ensure back-office or internal costs are excluded from artist-facing totals.

Session 4: Non-Shared Revenue, Additions, Deductions

Add Non-Shared Revenue

  • Declare and split revenue items not shared with artist (e.g., merch, bar sales).

Additions and Deductions

  • Use manual adjustments for settlement-specific items like penalties or bonuses.
